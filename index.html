Our Food and Desert

Akou cake

is a popular Khmer dessert, a sweet, yellow cake made with sticky rice and tapioca flours, typically topped with shredded mature coconut. It is considered a very delicious and attractive dessert that is well-liked by Cambodians and recommended to tourists.

Amok

Amok, specifically Amok Trey (Fish Amok), is Cambodia's national dish, a fragrant, creamy steamed fish curry with a mousse-like texture. It features fish, kroeung (a spice paste of lemongrass, galangal, turmeric, garlic, and herbs)

Num banhchok

Num banhchok are lightly fermented Cambodian rice noodles and a breakfast noodle dish. banhchok "បញ្ចុក" translates to "to feed" in Khmer language.

Fish food

Khmer soup, known as samlor or samlar, is a cornerstone of Cambodian cuisine, Aromatic spices like lemongrass, galangal, and kaffir lime leaves are a constant, while the famously pungent fermented fish paste, prahok, lends a rich, umami depth to many varieties.

OurCulture

Khmer woman

Historically, Khmer women have been viewed as the preservers of the home, tradition, and family finances, often expected to be modest and soft-spoken. While this traditional image still persists, modern Khmer women are increasingly active in politics, the economy, and traditionally male-dominated professions, although gender inequality continues to be a challenge.

Khmer man

In Cambodian (Khmer) culture, the concept of a "Cambodian man" is shaped by a mix of traditional gender roles, Buddhist values, family expectations, and modern influences. While generalizations are difficult, certain characteristics related to family, behavior, and appearance are common.

Khmer Wedding

Traditional Khmer weddings are elaborate, multi-day celebrations rich in symbolism, music, and tradition. Rooted in legend and Buddhist principles, he ceremonies emphasize the joining of two families, honor ancestors, and cleanse the couple for a fresh start. Though historically lasting up to seven days.
